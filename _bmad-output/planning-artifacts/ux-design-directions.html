Direction A
Sidebar + Detail panel Primary direction
Classic GNOME two-panel layout. Sync pairs list in sidebar with status dots; selected pair shown in detail panel with stats and actions. Persistent account header and status footer. Most information visible without interaction.

Direction B
Compact card list — no detail panel
All sync pairs displayed as cards in a single scrollable list. Each card shows paths, status pill, and last-sync time inline. No separate detail panel — all info visible at a glance. Better for users with many pairs; less suited for conflict management.

Direction C
Dashboard + Activity feed
Stats-first header showing aggregate sync metrics. Activity feed on the right shows recent file-level events. Good for users who want to see what just synced; more complex; may distract from the "invisible when healthy" principle.

Direction D
Wide sidebar with inline pair detail + conflict panel
Wider sidebar (280px) shows pair details inline — paths, progress bar, last-sync time — without needing a separate click. Right panel focuses on actionable items: conflicts, errors, recent changes. Efficient for power users managing multiple pairs.
